Guglioni Marconi gave us the radio.

The world has hardly advanced one inch in terms of technolgy since Guglioni Marconi debuted his wireless telegraph in 1895. In the past 120 years, the human race has been merely distracted by such whoopidy-dings as the silly television, debuted in 1927, and the Rube Goldberg of all time - the webcam, first seen in 1991. It is clear to this writer that since 1895, the human race has experienced the very worst of diminishing returns on his beloved technological advances benefiting society. I for one will continue to sit in front of my console, with my good ear cocked fondly towards the horn of my phonograph.
